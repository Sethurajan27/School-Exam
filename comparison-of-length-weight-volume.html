Comparison of Length, Weight, and Volume

We use length, weight, and volume to measure and compare different things around us. Let's learn what they mean and how to compare them!

Length

Length tells us how long or tall something is. We measure length in units like centimeters, meters, or inches.

For example, a pencil might be 15 centimeters long, and a table might be 1 meter long. We say the table is longer than the pencil.

Weight

Weight tells us how heavy or light something is. We measure weight in grams or kilograms.

A book might weigh 500 grams, while a watermelon might weigh 3 kilograms. The watermelon is heavier than the book.

Volume

Volume tells us how much space something takes up, especially liquids. We measure volume in liters or milliliters.

A small bottle may hold 500 milliliters of water, and a big jug may hold 2 liters. The jug holds more water than the bottle.

By comparing length, weight, and volume, we understand more about objects and their sizes or amounts.
