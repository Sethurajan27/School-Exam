Rain Drop

Rain drops are small drops of water that fall from the sky when clouds become heavy. When the sun heats up water in rivers, lakes, and oceans, the water turns into vapor and goes up into the sky. This vapor cools down to form clouds. When the clouds get heavy, rain drops fall to the ground.

We enjoy the rain because it makes the earth cool and fresh. Plants, animals, and people all need rainwater.

Example:
Rani looked out of the window and smiled as the tiny rain drops tapped on the glass. She ran outside with her umbrella and jumped in puddles.

Flowchart: How Rain Drops Form

Sun Heats Water → Water Turns to Vapor → Vapor Forms Clouds → Clouds Get Heavy → Rain Drops Fall

Diagram: Water Cycle

  • Evaporation: Water heats and rises as vapor
  • Condensation: Vapor cools and forms clouds
  • Precipitation: Rain falls as drops
  • Collection: Water gathers in rivers and lakes
Moral: Rain drops are nature’s way of watering the earth. Let’s use water wisely!
