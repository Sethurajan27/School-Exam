Articles: A, An, The

Articles are words that come before a noun. They help us know if we are talking about something specific or something general.

Types of Articles

  • A – used before words that begin with a consonant sound (e.g., a cat, a dog)
  • An – used before words that begin with a vowel sound (a, e, i, o, u) (e.g., an apple, an elephant)
  • The – used to talk about something specific or known (e.g., the sun, the car)
Examples:
- I saw a bird in the tree.
- She ate an orange.
- The moon is bright tonight.
- Please close the door.

Flowchart: Choosing the Right Article

Is the noun specific? → Yes → Use "The"
No → Does it start with a vowel sound? → Yes → Use "An"
No → Use "A"
