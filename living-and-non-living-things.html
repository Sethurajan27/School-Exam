Living and Non-Living Things

In our world, everything around us is either living or non-living. Understanding the difference helps us learn about nature and how things work.

Living things are those that grow, breathe, reproduce, and respond to their surroundings. They need food, water, and air to survive.

Example: Animals, plants, birds, insects, and humans are all living things.

Non-living things are things that do not grow, breathe, or reproduce. They do not need food or water and cannot move on their own.

Example: Rocks, water, air, chairs, and books are non-living things.

By observing and learning about living and non-living things, we can better understand the world around us.
