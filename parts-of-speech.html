Parts of Speech

Parts of speech are the different types of words that we use to build sentences. They help us understand how words work together.

Main Parts of Speech

  • Noun: Names of people, places, animals, or things. (e.g., dog, school, Mary)
  • Pronoun: Words that replace nouns. (e.g., he, she, it)
  • Verb: Action words or state of being. (e.g., run, is, jump)
  • Adjective: Words that describe nouns. (e.g., big, red, happy)
  • Adverb: Words that describe verbs or adjectives. (e.g., quickly, very)
  • Preposition: Shows the relationship between a noun/pronoun and another word. (e.g., in, on, under)
  • Conjunction: Connects words or sentences. (e.g., and, but, or)
  • Interjection: Shows emotion. (e.g., wow!, oh!)
Example Sentence:
She (pronoun) quickly (adverb) runs (verb) to the big (adjective) school (noun).

Flowchart: Understanding Parts of Speech

Word → Is it a person, place, or thing? → Yes → Noun
→ No → Does it show action or state? → Yes → Verb
→ No → Does it describe a noun? → Yes → Adjective
→ No → Other parts of speech
