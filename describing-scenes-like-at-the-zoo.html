Describing Scenes Like at the Zoo

When we visit the zoo, we see many different animals and their homes. Describing a scene means telling others what we see, hear, and feel in words.

We can describe animals, their colors, sounds, and actions. We also talk about the zoo environment, like trees, cages, and visitors.

Example:
“The tall giraffe stretched its long neck to eat the green leaves from the tree. Nearby, the playful monkeys swung from branch to branch, chattering loudly.”

Flowchart: Steps to Describe a Scene

Observe → Notice Details → Use Senses (See, Hear, Feel) → Choose Words → Describe Clearly

Diagram: What to Include in a Scene Description

  • Animals and Their Actions
  • Colors and Shapes
  • Sounds and Smells
  • Environment (Trees, Water, Visitors)
